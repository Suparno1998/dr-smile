Keeping You Safe During COVID-19
by Dr. Smile | 4 March 2020

Dr. Smile is happy to announce that, we are once again open for clinical appointments (fillings, crowns, implants, extractions, etc.)

Please contact us to book your appointment.

Patient safety has always been our top priority. We’ll continue to treat every patient with universal precautions and are taking extra measures to keep you and your family safe during this pandemic.

What we’re doing to keep you safe

Infection control has always been a top priority for our practice. In the midst of the current Coronavirus pandemic, we’d like to reassure you that our team continues to be committed to your health and well-being. 

We’re implementing extra precautions to exceed all mandated safety procedures, including:

1. Taking temperatures of every team member before the day begins.

2. Utilizing extra intraoral suction devices to decrease aerosols..

3. Wearing the best-available personal protective equipment (PPE), including custom-fitted masks and face shields to prevent unknown cross-contamination. We also wear clean patient gowns for each appointment.

What to expect at your next appointment

In addition to the extra sterilization and infection control procedures above, you may notice a few changes at your next appointment.

1. We’ll take your temperature..

2. We’re doing our best to reduce waiting times for you and reduce the number of patients in the reception area at any one time.

3. We proactively clean and disinfect our waiting rooms, patient rooms, and are limiting common “touch points,” like the computer registration pads etc.

4.  Hand sanitizer will be available at check-in and check-out — we’ll encourage you to use it!

5. Toys, books and magazines have been removed from our waiting rooms for infection control purposes.

6. We request that families limit non-essential adults during office visits. When possible, only bring the child/children being seen for a visit with one parent/guardian.

We’ve missed seeing you here in our office and are ready to safely provide you with the high-quality, comprehensive care you’ve come to expect.

The importance of good oral hygiene during the pandemic

Taking care of your dental health is always important — but it’s especially critical during this Coronavirus pandemic. There is a direct correlation to the health of the mouth and the overall body. Infection, inflammation, and other oral health issues have been linked to a variety of diseases.

Don’t let oral health issues affect your ability to fight off a virus and stay healthy. Our team is here to keep your teeth and gums healthy, reducing your risk of getting sick and improving your health during this vulnerable time.
